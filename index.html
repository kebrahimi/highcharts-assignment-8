Poverty Status of Families by Household in the United States

By Kassidy Ebrahimi

Source: U.S. Census Bureau, Table B-6

The United States has been affected by poverty increasing throughout the years. The last few years were updated by the United States Census Bureau on poverty thresholds. One, in particular, is below poverty, meaning if a family or individual is living below the poverty threshold, the family will be considered below the poverty line. There is a higher amount of below poverty line for female householders than males. Visit ASPE for more on poverty measurements.

Source: U.S. Census Bureau, Table B-6

There has been a slight increase in poverty throughout the years 2014 to 2020. Poverty, in general, can be caused by a multitude of factors. Human Rights Careers lists instances leading to poverty: employment, low salary, disabilities, education, government support, etc. The average poverty threshold for four is around $25,700, with women making up 12.9% of poverty-stricken households. For more information on poverty, go to Poverty USA.
